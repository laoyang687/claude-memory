# AI Application Case Filtering Guidelines

This document provides detailed criteria for identifying and selecting high-quality AI application news.

## What Qualifies as an "AI Application Case"?

An AI application case demonstrates **practical implementation** of AI technology to solve real-world problems.

### Core Requirements

1. **Concrete Implementation**: Not just research or theory
2. **Real Organization**: Named company, institution, or project
3. **Specific Use Case**: Clear problem being solved
4. **AI Technology Identified**: What type of AI is being used

## Inclusion Criteria

### ✅ High Priority (Always Include)

**Enterprise AI Deployments**
- Company implements AI for business operations
- Example: "Walmart uses computer vision AI to monitor inventory in real-time"

**Product Launches with AI Features**
- New products/services powered by AI
- Example: "Adobe launches AI-powered image editing tool for automatic background removal"

**Industry-Specific Solutions**
- AI solving domain-specific problems
- Example: "Hospital reduces diagnosis time by 40% using AI pathology assistant"

**Measurable Results**
- Cases with quantifiable outcomes
- Example: "Manufacturing plant cuts defects by 25% with AI quality control"

**Open Source AI Applications**
- Practical tools/frameworks being adopted
- Example: "Stable Diffusion integration enables real-time game asset generation"

### ✅ Medium Priority (Include if Relevant)

**AI Tool Adoption Trends**
- Significant adoption statistics
- Example: "ChatGPT reaches 100M users in 2 months - fastest app ever"

**Regulatory/Policy Implementation**
- AI governance with practical impact
- Example: "EU AI Act requires risk assessments for high-risk AI systems"

**AI Infrastructure Developments**
- Platform/infrastructure enabling applications
- Example: "AWS launches new AI inference chips, reducing costs by 50%"

**Partnership Announcements**
- If they specify concrete use cases
- Example: "Mercedes partners with Nvidia to build AI-powered autonomous driving system"

### ❌ Exclude

**Pure Research**
- Academic papers without implementation
- "Researchers propose new neural architecture..." (unless deployed)

**Vague Announcements**
- "Company exploring AI opportunities"
- "AI will revolutionize industry X" (without specifics)

**Funding News Without Product Details**
- "Startup raises $10M for AI" (unless product is described)

**Opinion/Analysis Pieces**
- Commentary without new information
- Predictions without current applications

**Duplicate Content**
- Same news from multiple sources (pick best source)

**Outdated News**
- Implementations from >6 months ago (unless historically significant)

## Quality Indicators

### Strong Signals
- **Specific numbers**: "30% improvement", "1M users"
- **Named entities**: Company names, product names, specific industries
- **Technical details**: AI model types, deployment scale
- **Timeframes**: "launched today", "deployed last month"
- **Evidence**: Screenshots, demos, case studies

### Weak Signals
- **Vague language**: "may", "could", "planning to"
- **No specifics**: "AI solutions", "advanced technology"
- **Marketing speak**: "revolutionary", "game-changing" (without proof)
- **No attribution**: Unnamed sources, no company confirmation

## Content Evaluation Checklist

For each news item, ask:

1. ☐ Is this about a real, deployed application? (not just research/concept)
2. ☐ Can I identify the specific organization using/building it?
3. ☐ Do I understand what problem it solves?
4. ☐ Is the AI technology specified? (CV, NLP, LLM, etc.)
5. ☐ Are there concrete details or results?
6. ☐ Is this recent? (within last week for daily digest)
7. ☐ Will readers find this actionable or insightful?

If 5+ answers are "yes", **include it**.
If 3-4 answers are "yes", **include only if space permits**.
If <3 answers are "yes", **exclude**.

## Regional Considerations

### Global Sources
- Focus on widely applicable use cases
- Include major product launches
- Highlight cross-border adoptions

### China-Specific
- Domestic AI applications often underreported in Western media
- Include Chinese company innovations (Baidu, Alibaba, Tencent, etc.)
- Note regulatory/policy differences

### Domain-Specific Deep Dives
When user requests specific domain (e.g., "healthcare AI"):
- Lower the bar slightly for niche applications
- Include early-stage pilots if they show promise
- Provide more technical detail on implementation

## Example Evaluations

### ✅ INCLUDE
**Title**: "DoorDash rolls out AI-powered delivery routing, cutting wait times by 15%"
- ✓ Real company (DoorDash)
- ✓ Specific application (delivery routing)
- ✓ AI technology (routing optimization)
- ✓ Measurable result (15% reduction)
- ✓ Deployed (not planned)

### ❌ EXCLUDE
**Title**: "AI will transform the food delivery industry, experts say"
- ✗ Opinion piece, not implementation
- ✗ No specific company or product
- ✗ No concrete details

### ✅ INCLUDE
**Title**: "Chinese hospital deploys Ali Health's AI assistant for triage"
- ✓ Real organization (named hospital + Ali Health)
- ✓ Specific use case (patient triage)
- ✓ Deployed system
- ✓ Healthcare application case

### ❌ EXCLUDE
**Title**: "Startup SecretAI raises $5M to build next-gen AI platform"
- ✗ Funding announcement only
- ✗ No product description
- ✗ "Next-gen" is vague
- Note: Include if article describes their specific product/use case

## Prioritization When Limit Reached

If you need to cut down to top items, prioritize:

1. **Impact & Scale**: Large deployments, major companies
2. **Novelty**: First-of-its-kind applications
3. **Results**: Quantifiable outcomes
4. **Relevance**: Matches user's domain interests
5. **Source Quality**: Established publications over blogs

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

- Don't confuse "AI-powered" marketing claims with real AI usage
- Don't include vendor case studies without verification
- Don't repeat news that's already well-known (e.g., ChatGPT launch)
- Don't include too many similar cases (pick the best example)

## Summary

**Goal**: Curate actionable, substantive AI application news that helps readers understand how AI is being used in practice today.

**Principle**: Quality over quantity. 5 strong cases > 20 vague mentions.
